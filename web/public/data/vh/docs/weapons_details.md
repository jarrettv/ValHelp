# Weapon Details

Per-weapon notes that appear on the detail screen between the recipe and the properties. Each section is keyed to an item by an HTML comment containing the item code on the `###` heading.

Format:

    ### Iron Sword <!-- SwordIron -->
    Notes here. Supports **bold**, *italic*, bullet lists, and tables.

`##` category headers are for author organization — they are not rendered on the detail screen. Only `### Title <!-- Code -->` sections are extracted.

## Unarmed

### Paws of the Bear <!-- FistBjornClaw -->

The **best** early weapon in the game! The {parry:6} parry and the left hook (2nd punch) is incredibly powerful. The secondary **kick** attack will stagger nearly everything.

Well worth killing two bears early or just one if you are lucky to find a ★ bear.

## Swords

### Wooden Sword <!-- SwordWood -->

**Did you know?** If you trap a bear near a fire, you can use the wooden sword to level-up quickly. Just eat 3 {fork:sta} stam foods and go to town. You'll be at level 12 in no time.

### Bronze Sword <!-- SwordBronze -->

The first sword is pretty powerful and upgrades well. It can be effective in the first 5 biomes especially with good sword skill.

All 1-handed swords have a primary attack 3-hit combo where the last hit does double damage. The secondary attack stab has a longer reach and better stagger.



### Mistwalker <!-- SwordMistwalker -->

Probably the best sword in the game since few mobs resist Frost and it gives the slowing effect. Curiously, upgrading adds only Frost and Spirit damage. That Spirit damage doesn't help in Mistlands but is great in the Ashlands.

All 1-handed swords have a primary attack 3-hit combo where the last hit does double damage. The secondary attack stab has a longer reach and better stagger.


### Silver Sword <!-- SwordSilver -->

The amount of silver needed to fully upgrade is bonkers. However, this sword will melt mobs that don't resist the Spirit damage. You can take this sword into the Ashlands.

All 1-handed swords have a primary attack 3-hit combo where the last hit does double damage. The secondary attack stab has a longer reach and better stagger.


## Axes

### Early Axes <!-- AxeEarly -->

Added 5 years after the game released to "Early Access" as a pun joke. These dual-wield axes are OP if you can find the axe heads. They even chop birch and oak trees allowing you to skip the [AxeBronze]* Bronze Axe.

You need to find the [AxeHead1]* curious axe head in the 2x2 tall houses or the [AxeHead2]* mysterious axe head in the larger A-frame house. The houses can be found randomly in the meadows but you won't find them in the farm houses.


## Clubs and Maces

### Stagbreaker <!-- SledgeStagbreaker -->

The Stagbreaker is a useful weapon for burial chambers, sunken crypts, and frost caves. The AoE hits in an 8 meter sphere that can **damage mobs behind doors/walls** and below you as well.

For example, you can safely destroy the spawners in sunken crypts.

Bring with you to the plains for quickly stealing flax and barley from fueling villages.

Like all 2-handed clubs, it only has a primary attack that slams the ground knocking everything in an 8m sphere backwards. Very useful for dealing Blunt damage above and below you.



### Frostner <!-- MaceSilver -->

Requires [YmirRemains](5) which you can buy from Haldor for 600 coins total. The unique traits of this weapon make it very effective against groups of enemies. The Frostner is considered one of the best weapons in the game.

The Spirit damage does well in the Ashlands and the Frost damage will slow most enemies.

Upgrades are expensive but you gain useful Frost damage which does extra damage to wet enemies. Just remember that both the Frost and Spirit damage don't contribute to stagger.

Like all clubs, the primary attack is a 3 swing combo with the 3rd overhead hit doing 2x damage and some 20% extra knockback. The secondary attack is a long underhanded swing that does 2.5x damage and 2x staggering. The secondary attack has great knockback too.

## Tools and Misc

### Dyrnwyn <!-- SwordDyrnwyn -->

You need three unique fragments to craft this sword. These fragments are found at mysterious locations in the Ashlands. You can sometimes (25% chance) find a Vegvisir inside Putrid Holes that marks the first location.

The mini-boss [Lord Reto](/guides/enemies/LordReto) protects the third location.

Although this is the most powerful one-handed sword in the game, the difficulty in crafting this weapon combined with minimal gains in attack power make it hardly worth it. Perhaps this sword and the fire effect will own the Deep North.






### Flesh Rippers <!-- FistFenrirClaw -->

A fantastic and highly durable weapon that pairs well with the Fenris set bonus. These gauntlets are the strongest unarmed slash weapon.

The 2nd punch of the primary attack does 2x damage. Use the secondary kick attack to stagger most enemies.



### Vilebone Maulclaws <!-- FistBjornUndeadClaw -->

The best unarmed weapon in the game but only if the enemy is vulnerable to both slash and pierce. This weapon does more pierce than slash damage.

Oddly, it has the same block power as the other unarmed weapons so don't assume these claws can parry stronger enemies.

Remember, the 2nd punch of the primary attack does 2x damage. The secondary kick attack will stagger most enemies prior to Ashlands.

### Slayer <!-- THSwordSlayer -->

This crazy powerful, but expensive, 2-handed sword can be upgraded with gems.

**WARNING** Don't upgrade this sword if you plan to "enchant" it with a gem. Any upgrade levels are lost when used to craft another weapon.

[GemstoneRed]* + [THSwordSlayer]* = [THSwordSlayerBlood]* [Brutal Slayer](/guides/weapons/swords/THSwordSlayerBlood)
[GemstoneGreen]* + [THSwordSlayer]* = [THSwordSlayerNature]* [Primal Slayer](/guides/weapons/swords/THSwordSlayerNature)
[GemstoneBlue]* + [THSwordSlayer]* = [THSwordSlayerLightning]* [Scourging Slayer](/guides/weapons/swords/THSwordSlayerLightning)

For primary attack, the 3rd hit is more narrow but will do 2x damage and 20% extra knockback.

The secondary attack is a long reaching stab motion that is far more effective at staggering the enemy.















### Brutal Slayer <!-- THSwordSlayerBlood -->

As a bloodstone weapon, this weapon's damage increases by 0.2% for every point of HP the player is missing.

### Primal Slayer <!-- THSwordSlayerNature -->

As a jade weapon, this 2-handed sword has a 20% chance to apply a root effect that immobilizes the enemy.

### Scourging Slayer <!-- THSwordSlayerLightning -->

Like other iolite weapons, this 2-handed sword has additional lightning damage and a 25% chance to apply chain-lightning which can also damage nearby mobs.

This is probably the best 2-handed sword in the game.

### Krom <!-- THSwordKrom -->

The first 2-handed sword is expensive to craft, but will give you incredible attack power. Combine with [MeadHasty]* Tonic of Ratatosk to dominate your foes.

For the primary attack, the 3rd hit is more narrow but will do 2x damage and 20% extra knockback.

The secondary attack is a long reaching stab motion that is far more effective at staggering the enemy.

### Stone Axe <!-- AxeStone -->

Usually the first axe you'll craft and you don't even need a workbench. Very cheap to upgrade which you should do ASAP.

Like all 1-handed axes, the primary attack is a 3-hit combo with 2x damage on the last hit. Its secondary attack is a slow but powerful overhead swing, perfect for removing tree stumps or hitting enemies below you. For example, you can damage a Leech from a raft.

### Flint Spear <!-- SpearFlint -->

The go-to weapon for tearing thru the beginning of the game. A favorite of speedrunners for high DPS and low stamina usage. A level 4 spear will make quick work of a Troll and can even stagger Draugr.

Like all spears, the primary attack is a quick stabbing motion. For the secondary attack, you can throw the spear for ranged damage. You must aim above your target but spears always fly true.

### Bronze Spear <!-- SpearBronze -->

This spear is commonly skipped in favor of the [SpearElderBark]* Ancient Bark Spear. Spears are generally cheap but this one is expensive because bronze is expensive to craft.

Like all spears, the primary attack is a quick stabbing motion. For the secondary attack, you can throw the spear for ranged damage. You must aim above your target but spears always fly true.

### Ancient Bark Spear <!-- SpearElderbark -->

A favorite spear of speedrunners because, like most spears, it is relatively cheap to craft and offers amazing DPS per stamina usage. If you master the spear throw, you can even forego a bow.

The only gotcha is that this weapon requires higher Forge levels than most iron-tier weapons.

Like all spears, the primary attack is a quick stabbing motion. For the secondary attack, you can throw the spear for ranged damage. You must aim above your target but spears always fly true.


### Fang Spear <!-- SpearWolfFang -->

An incredibly cheap yet powerful spear. If you have the materials, this is a no-brainer. You'll wreck all biomes until the Ashlands.

**WARNING** throwing spears around on a mountain is an easy way to lose your spear. Perhaps this is why they made it so cheap to craft.

Like all spears, the primary attack is a quick stabbing motion. For the secondary attack, you can throw the spear for ranged damage. You must aim above your target but spears always fly true.

### Carapace Spear <!-- SpearCarapace -->

Another common favorite of speedrunners because of how much DPS you can do for such little stamina. Typically you'd spend [Mandible](2) on this rather than the Carapace Helmet but they are both solid choices.

With stronger enemies, you can usually cheese them with the ranged attack to stay out of harm's way.

Like all spears, the primary attack is a quick stabbing motion. For the secondary attack, you can throw the spear for ranged damage. You must aim above your target but spears always fly true.

### Flint Knife <!-- KnifeFlint -->

The first knife is really nice because of the low stamina usage and lack of a speed penalty.

Knives level up extremely quickly and the backstab can one-shot trolls at the right level.

Like most knives, you get balanced Slash + Pierce damage. They also have a strong {parry:4} parry or if you sneak up on an enemy, you get 6x "backstab" damage. The primary attack is a fast 3-slash combo with 2x damage on the 3rd swing. The secondary attack is a leaping stab with 3x damage.




### Copper Knife <!-- KnifeCopper -->

A solid upgrade over the flint knife and fairly cheap as it only uses copper.

Like most knives, you get balanced Slash + Pierce damage. They also have a strong {parry:4} parry or if you sneak up on an enemy, you get 6x "backstab" damage. The primary attack is a fast 3-slash combo with 2x damage on the 3rd swing. The secondary attack is a leaping stab with 3x damage.

### Club <!-- Club -->

Typically the very first weapon to craft is a solid starter Blunt weapon. You can craft it without a workbench and it will upgrade well over time. This club will get you thru the Black Forest and into the Swamp.

Like all clubs, the primary attack is a 3 swing combo with the 3rd overhead hit doing 2x damage and some 20% extra knockback.

Unlike other clubs, this club has no secondary attack.



### Bronze Mace <!-- MaceBronze -->

This mace has some decent block power but is somewhat expensive compared to crafting a club. However, low stamina usage makes it pretty easy to smash your foes with Blunt damage.

Like all clubs, the primary attack is a 3 swing combo with the 3rd overhead hit doing 2x damage and some 20% extra knockback. The secondary attack is a long underhanded swing that does 2.5x damage and 2x staggering. The secondary attack has great knockback too.

### Iron Mace <!-- MaceIron -->

This mace is great in the Swamp and stays effective in the next couple biomes. Blunt damage is pretty effective against most enemies.

This mace is relatively cheap to craft and you only need a level 2 Forge.
